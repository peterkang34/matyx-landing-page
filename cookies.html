Cookie Policy

Matyx Agency LLC · Last updated July 21, 2026

What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They let a site recognize your device, remember your preferences, and understand how the site is used. Similar technologies — such as pixels and web beacons — work the same way. This policy covers all of them, and applies to matyx.com, operated by Matyx Agency LLC ("Matyx," "we," "us").

How we use them

We use cookies to keep the site working properly, remember your choices, understand which pages are most useful, and measure the effectiveness of our marketing. Cookies cannot run programs or deliver viruses to your device, and can only be read by the domain that set them.

Types of cookies we use

TypeWhat it does
Strictly necessary Required for the site to function — security, page delivery, and remembering actions within a browsing session. These can't be switched off through our site.
Analytics Help us understand how visitors use the site — which pages are visited, how long visitors stay, and where they come from. We use tools such as Google Analytics for this.
Functionality Remember choices you make — such as preferences and settings — so the site works the way you left it between visits.
Advertising Set by third-party platforms (such as Google, Meta, or LinkedIn) to measure our campaigns and show you relevant ads on other sites based on your visit here. These providers have their own privacy policies.

Third-party cookies & embeds

Some cookies are set by third parties through our site — for example, analytics providers, advertising platforms, and embedded content such as YouTube videos. We use privacy-enhanced embeds where available, but third-party services operate under their own privacy and cookie policies, which we encourage you to review.

Email pixels

Emails we send may include a small pixel that tells us whether the email was opened or a link was clicked. We use this to make our communications more useful and less frequent — not more. Most email clients let you block remote images, which disables these pixels.

Managing cookies

You can refuse or delete cookies through your browser settings at any time. If you do, some parts of the site may not work as intended. Unless your browser is set to refuse them, our system will issue cookies when you visit. Instructions for common browsers are available in each browser's help documentation, and you can opt out of Google Analytics specifically via Google's opt-out browser add-on.

Retention

Data collected through cookies is kept no longer than necessary for the purposes described above, and in any event no longer than one year.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
